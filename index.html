Integrating African Perspectives into Technology and Design
A Mozilla Responsible Computing Project

Students' Design Projects

A significant part of the course was devoted to using human-centered design (HCD) to develop a design intervention. Students drew from the readings, class discussions, assignments, and conversations with TIR and guest lecturers to identify a design problem. They then used the HCD process to explore possible technical solutions. They began their project by sketching various design concepts and building low-fidelity prototypes. Groups then worked with TIR to develop a 'design brief' to share with the Kenyan designers.

Afterward, student groups collaborated with these designers to develop a high-fidelity prototype that effectively communicated their design concept and how it could potentially address the chosen problem. This process required meeting with the Kenyan designers at least once (via Zoom). Groups documented their design process, which was incorporated into an end-of-semester final report and presentation.

Kenyan Collaborators

During the course, Michigan State University students collaborated with 'Technologists in Residence' and Kenyan designers to better understand the context in which they were designing technologies and to develop their design concepts.

Technologists in Residence (TIRS)

The Technologists-in-Residence Program (TIR) consists of students from Kenya’s Egerton University. During the course, they served as mentors, evaluated MSU students’ projects, and contributed a cross-cultural perspective. They participated in the course virtually for most of the semester and visited campus in April 2024 to attend the project showcase.
